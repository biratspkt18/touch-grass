# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People (mostly young, phone-first) who want to get out of the house but don't know where to go. They open the app on a whim — on the couch, on a walk, visiting a new suburb — to find somewhere worth going right now. Contributors are the same people in "share" mode: they just found a great spot and want to pin it under their name.

## Product Purpose

Touch Grass is a social map of places worth leaving the house for — parks, cafés, viewpoints, food, anything local. Success: a user opens the app, sees the map or the near-you feed, and walks out the door to one of the spots. Browsing is open to everyone; pinning and commenting require an account (Google or email).

## Brand Personality

Minimal yet whimsical — a calm, airy, photo-forward interface with small moments of play (a rounded display face for big headings, a rare emoji, a friendly empty state). Whimsy is a seasoning, not the dish. Three words: fresh, unhurried, friendly.

## Anti-references

- The previous iteration of this very app: green gradient banners on every screen, emoji in every title, brand color everywhere. Green is now for primary actions and selection only.
- Loud gamified social apps (streaks, badges, confetti).
- Sterile enterprise dashboards — minimal must not become joyless.

## Design Principles

1. **Photos and the map are the color.** Chrome stays white/neutral; content provides the vibrance (Airbnb is the reference).
2. **One accent, spent deliberately.** Brand green marks the primary action and the selected state — never decoration.
3. **Map first, feed near.** The app opens on the map; the feed answers "what's new near me".
4. **Whimsy in moments.** Rounded display type on big headings, warm copy in empty states. Never gradients-by-default or emoji-by-default.
5. **Same vocabulary everywhere.** One button shape, one card shape, one chip shape across all screens.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast on its background; no gray-on-tint.
- Tap targets ≥ 44pt; hitSlop where controls are visually small.
- Works one-handed: primary actions bottom-center or thumb-reachable.
